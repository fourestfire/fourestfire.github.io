The TLDR:

School is surprisingly tough academically, and there is a lot of random social stuff / extracurriculars, but it's a lot of fun. I'm living with two other first years in North Berkeley, and our class has ~280 people. We're then divided up into 4 cohorts. I'm in Gold (the best cohort), and within that, I'm in a 6 person study team that meets every week.

Careerwise, I'm trying to recruit for product management positions at big tech companies. A lot of this happens in the spring, so there's still a bit of time before then, but ideally we need to start reaching out to alumni and companies in the fall.

People are very smart here, and I feel very average, which is perhaps an amazing thing as there is a lot of room to grow. I'm being exposed to a lot of people with different backgrounds. There are a lot of consulting and finance people, but within my cohort, we also have an ex-Marine focused on anti-terrorism, a professional cook who has appeared on Food network, and a lot of people who've done really cool social impact work.
